Robotics

The Robotics and Multi-Agent Systems research center is established to foster research in the robotics application of:

  • Multi agent Systems
  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Fuzzy Logic Control
  • Constraint Programming
  • And Computer Vision

The RMAS research center offers research opportunities to both postgraduate and undergraduate students. The RMAS research center targets its research towards the RoboCup Competition as a means of participating in international research.

Computer Vision & Graphics

In the digital era, digital imaging is rapidly growing to become an essential medium to retrieve, display and manipulate information. Digital imaging is the core around which different fields of computer research are established. Such fields include Computer Vision, Image Processing, Computer Graphics and Visualization. Although digital imaging can be considered a greatest common divisor among those fields, essential differences are present among them. For instance, while Computer Graphics is the field that is concerned with representing descriptive data through artificial digital imaging form, Computer Vision is the field that is concerned with extracting descriptive data from real-world digital images. Another closely related and important research field is Image Processing that is concerned with manipulation of digital images. Algorithms developed in this field may be used as pre-processing steps to enhance the outcome of a vision system. All the four fields; i.e., Computer Vision, Image Processing, Computer Graphics and Visualization, are connected to some extent

Mobile and Pervasive Computing

Vehicular ad-hoc network (VANET) is a special pattern of the conventional mobile ad hoc network (MANET), where nodes consist of vehicles equipped with wireless units for inter-vehicle or vehicle/road side infrastructure communication. Recently, VANETs gained lots of the researchers’ attention because of their ability to provide many services ranging from road safety to road comfort. However, due to their special characteristics, providing such services is a challenging and complex task.

Data dissemination is a major challenge in providing such services since communication between vehicles is mostly based on data broadcasting causing the “broadcast storm” phenomena. Our interest is mainly in providing an efficient and robust way for data dissemination among drivers.

We aim at tackling the following questions:
  1. Which information to disseminate:
    • Data ranking
    • Data aggregation
  2. Who need this information
    • Users interests
  3. How to disseminate this information
    • Efficient routing
